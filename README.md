#  U.S. Police Shootings Dashboard
An interactive analytics dashboard built in Microsoft Excel to explore fatal police shooting incidents in the United States, examining victim demographics, threat levels, mental illness involvement, and geographic patterns. 

# Project Overview
Understanding fatal police shooting incidents requires looking beyond headline counts to the underlying patterns — who is involved, where incidents occur, and the circumstances surrounding each event. This project analyzes 4,895 U.S. police shooting records to uncover trends across race, threat level, mental illness status, body camera usage, and time.

The dashboard enables users to interactively filter and explore key metrics across four dedicated views: **Overview, Race, Mental Illness, and Threat Level**.

# Business Problem
Making sense of police shooting data requires understanding several key factors, including victim demographics, the circumstances of each encounter, and how incidents are distributed across states and time.

This dashboard helps answer questions such as:
- How many incidents occurred, and how are they trending over time?
- Which racial groups are most represented in the data, and at what rates?
- How often is mental illness a factor, and does it change the outcome?
- What share of incidents are classified as an "attack" threat level versus other circumstances?
- How common is body camera usage, and does it vary by state or threat level?
- Which states and demographic groups show the highest incident counts?


# Objectives
- Clean and prepare the police shootings dataset
- Analyze incident trends by demographics, threat level, and mental illness status
- Compare states and age groups
- Evaluate the relationship between fleeing behavior, threat level, and body camera usage
- Build an interactive dashboard for incident analysis

# Business Insights
Based on the underlying pivot analysis, this dashboard reveals:
- **4,895 incidents** recorded across **51 states/territories**, with an average victim age of **~36.5 years**
- **Gender split:** 4,673 male victims vs. 222 female victims (≈95.5% male)
- **Race breakdown:** White (50.6%), Black (26.5%), Hispanic (18.4%), Asian (1.9%), Native American (1.6%), Other (1.0%)
- **Threat level:** 64.6% of incidents were classified as "attack," with the remainder split between "other" and "undetermined"
- **Mental illness:** signs of mental illness were present in roughly 22.5% of incidents (1,103 of 4,895)
- **Body camera usage:** only ~11.8% of incidents had a body camera in use
- **Fleeing behavior:** ~62.8% of victims were not fleeing at the time of the incident
- **Top states by incident count:** California (701), Texas (426), Florida (324), Arizona (222), Colorado (168), Georgia (161)
- **Manner of death:** the vast majority of incidents (4,647) were classified simply as "Shot," with a smaller share (248) as "Shot and Tasered"
